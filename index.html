Curiosity is the trademark of a creative person. I have always been interested in the world around me, something that has translated well in Tech. It took me a while to discover my passion for Web Design, which then sparked me to pursue a Masters degree in IT.

I was first interested in Psychology, Business and Leadership and Criminal Justice. A few years ago it dawned on me however, that I could not work in anything other than the tech industry. This epiphany was liberating and exciting! I thrust myself into the world of Web Design wholeheartedly absorbing every bit of information I can and gladly accepting guidance from professionals in the tech industry. I am happy that I spent many years working in other fields, I learned a lot and all of those experiences have shaped who I am today. Were I to discard those experiences, I would be incomplete.

My education initially placed me in management roles. While at CVS, I was promoted several times due to my exceptional track record of revenue and profitability growth, as well as team leadership. I left CVS to finish my Master's degree and focus on my true passion: Web Design. While business leadership experience does not directly translate to IT, it is nonetheless cruical in understanding company culture, leadership structure, organizational behavior and working in teams.

I gained web design experience during Graduate School at Southern New Hampshire Univeristy and my Internship at PixelOne Digital LLC (P1). At P1 I was able to successfully leverage development tools and frameworks to create websites and manage web services for company clients. I was exposed to the daily routines of a busy tech office. I learned to collaborate with team members through version control software and work on projects with industry-standard tools. I learned to utilize Photoshop in order to tailor media for projects and satisfy client requirements. Furthermore, I was able to advance my knowledge of Wordpress CMS, HTML and CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, Bootstrap, Sass, and PHP. During my time at P1 I was challenged with deadlines, asked to produce content, design and marketing deliverables, meet with clients to discuss design goals and needs, and collaborate with members of a highly talented tech team on a variety of challenges. In the process, I was able to build confidence and proficiency in the tools of the trade of Web Design and Development.

The greatest take-away from my internship at P1 is that my previous experience in business management and development is a substantial asset to my future in the Tech Industry. To be successful in Tech today means, that it is not enough to just know language syntax, frameworks or libraries. To be successful today requires the ability to collaborate with people, not just computers, to demonstrate ideas concisely and cost effectiely, successfully leverage soft skills, and aggresively pursue opportunities when they can; save money, build consensus, eliminate bottlenecks and shorten deadlines.

Recent Projects

The following is a list of my recent Website projects. These projects highlight my knowledge with HTML, CSS, Sass, Bootstrap and Photoshop as well as demonstrating my understanding of JavaScript (and to a lesser extent the JavaScript Library jQuery) Implementation in the form of scripts and plugins.

add

RNG Energy Solutions

The RNG Energy Solutions was created in 2017. I Co-Authored this website with the PixelOne Digital Team. It was one of my first experiences working with the Bootstrap framework. I was able to utilize Gulp as a development platform, task automator and Sass compiler. I wrote a large chunk of the CSS and HTML as well as designed most of the content and interactive elements.

The website is 100% responsive across all common devices. I cannot say enough about how fortunate I was to work with such a talented team as PixelOne Digital who have contributed greatly to my development as a Web Designer.

RNG Website
add

Phearson Design

This website is a Wordpress experiment, plain and simple. My friend often jokes that most designers start by learning the Wordpress CMS, unlike me. I started my journey by learning specifics about CSS and HTML and Frameworks like Bootstrap. The Wordpress CMS is powerful and straightforward, and extremely popular.

Phearson Design is a fictional company that I conjured up as a creative project. I designed the logo and wrote all the content, as well as some of the PHP source-code. I implemented this Instance of a Wordpress theme onto my own GoDaddy Server, where I learned to host and manage a Wordpress site from scratch. Working with Wordpress has taught me how to find and edit source-code files, work with wordpress plugins, and create child themes.

Phearson Design Website
add

Organic Bonita Studio

The Organic Bonita Site was co-authored by me and the Lead designer of PixelOne Digital LLC. After the project scaffolding was set up I began adding and porting over the site content from the previous website. I again had the opportunity to write original CSS and HTML, as well as implementing JavaScript for smooth-scroll and nav-menu animation.

The framework used for this site was Bootstrap 4 Alpha 6. Alpha 6 required a lot of editing and work-arounds as it is still unfinished. Gulp was used as a task automator and Sass Compiler. Photoshop was used for branding and image editing.

Organic Bonita Website
add

Crazy Goat Restaurant

The Crazy Goat website is a completely independent project that I completed myself for commercial use. I utilized everything I learned from my previous projects to create a mobile responsive, static website that looked good and worked well. The website was completed in just under a week from inception to release.

Gulp was used once again as well as Bootstrap for the main grid components. This project solidified my understanding of responsive design. I also had the opportunity to expand my knowledge of Sass by utilizing mixins.

Crazy Goat Website
